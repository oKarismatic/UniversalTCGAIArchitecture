Universal card game teaching Model
------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 1 — FOUNDATION (R, A, Z + S intro)
Goal: Understand legal actions, card movement, and timing basics

Layers:
  R = Rules (turns, summoning, win conditions)
  A = Accessibility (costs/restrictions)
  Z = Zone Movement (draw, discard, send to graveyard)
  S = Sequencing (introduced lightly: "order of actions matters even for basic moves")

Player Focus:
  "What can I legally do, where do cards go, and does order matter?"

Exercises:
  - Play basic cards, track movement, observe turn structure
  - Note if moving cards triggers anything (conditional or replacement effects)
  - Introduce "S-light": when two things happen simultaneously, which first?
  
Special Notes:
  - Timing awareness is introduced early to prevent missed trigger misunderstandings
------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 2 — ACCESS & STABILITY (C, H, B + S reinforcement)
Goal: Understand how to execute game plans reliably and efficiently

Layers:
  C = Consistency (drawing/searching key cards)
  H = Hand Advantage (options in hand)
  B = Board Advantage (control of field)
  S = Sequencing reinforcement ("order affects success of your combo or response")

Player Focus:
  "How do I get my strategy online and keep it running, considering timing?"

Exercises:
  - Identify deck bricking, test card synergy
  - Manage board presence
  - Note when play order affects outcome (early use of S)
  
Special Notes:
  - Introduce targeting vs non-targeting effects (important for Yu-Gi-Oh)
  - Start basic S-awareness for chains and interactions
------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 3 — INTERACTION & DEFENSE (N, M + Replacement Effects)
Goal: Introduce conflict, response, and pre/post resolution mechanics

Layers:
  N = Negation (prevent opponent actions, pre-resolution)
  M = Removal (eliminate opponent threats, post-resolution)
  S = Sequencing importance emphasized

Player Focus:
  "How do I stop or respond to threats? Does order or replacement effect matter?"

Exercises:
  - Decide when to counter or remove
  - Track replacement effects (if "would happen, do this instead")
  - Practice timing windows: If/When/Whenever

Special Notes:
  - Explain missed timing (triggers that can fail if not chained properly)
  - Multi-effect interactions introduced (I preview)
------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 4 — STRATEGY & COMBOS (V, T₁, I)
Goal: Understand win conditions, combo efficiency, and value scaling

Layers:
  V = Value / Win Conditions
  T₁ = Tempo (speed of converting advantage)
  I = Interaction / Combo Multiplier

Player Focus:
  "Which plays advance my win condition fastest? How do interactions multiply value?"

Exercises:
  - Analyze 2–3 card combos, calculate advantage
  - Optimize sequencing using S (previously learned)
  - Experiment with timing modifiers (If/When/Quick/Reactive)

Special Notes:
  - Introduce recursion and loops
  - Emphasize that I does not create advantage alone but multiplies existing layers
------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 5 — ADVANCED TIMING & SEQUENCING (S deep dive)
Goal: Master chain resolution, stack order, and advanced timing

Layers:
  S = Sequencing (order of actions matters)
  
Player Focus:
  "Correct actions in the wrong order are still wrong. Learn stack/chain/priority."

Exercises:
  - Practice chain resolution scenarios
  - Introduce conditional triggers (If/When/Whenever)
  - Explore replacement effects in complex sequences
  - Observe pre- and post-resolution dependencies (N vs M)

Special Notes:
  - Reinforce that S affects all layers equally
  - Timing-sensitive edge cases (Yu-Gi-Oh, MTG replacement effects)
------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 6 — META AWARENESS (M2, A2, O, T₂)
Goal: Understand the broader game environment

Layers:
  M2 = Meta Distribution (common decks/archetypes)
  A2 = Adaptation (tech choices, counters)
  O = Outcome Layer (matchup trends, win rates)
  T₂ = Meta Evolution Stage (new, shifting, solved)

Player Focus:
  "The game is an ecosystem; my deck is part of it"

Exercises:
  - Study matchups, test counters, track meta trends
  - Reflect on how timing and sequencing affect outcomes
  - Evaluate combos in context of meta prevalence

Special Notes:
  - Introduce continuous feedback loop: practice layers → evaluate → adapt
------------------------------------------------------------
GENERAL NOTES FOR TEACHERS & LEARNERS:
- Introduce sequencing (S) as early as possible, first lightly in Stage 1.
- Introduce targeting vs non-targeting effects in Stage 2.
- Replacement effects taught at Stage 3 with N/M context.
- Z always neutral; I multiplies layers; S affects order, not advantage.
- Beginner progression remains: Foundation → Access → Interaction → Strategy → Sequencing → Meta.
- Each game profile can define unique behavior, preserving identity and edge cases.
------------------------------------------------------------
END OF ZERO-AMBIGUITY TEACHING MODEL
